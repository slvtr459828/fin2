## III.1. Two Pillars

The optimization strategy rests on two pillars: sector-level momentum identification and technical entry timing.

**Pillar I: Sector Momentum Identification.** During the 20/5–5/6/2026 window, a pronounced sector rotation channelled institutional and foreign capital into Banking—the only sector with positive returns:

Among six major sectors, only Banking posted a positive return. Capital should concentrate where momentum is strongest. ACB, as the bank with the highest CAR ratio (13%+), was the strongest constituent. The SBV credit room expansion directly benefits ACB—a higher CAR translates into greater capacity to deploy incremental credit. Additionally, FTSE Emerging Market upgrade expectations catalyse foreign institutional inflows into ACB, which holds the largest foreign ownership headroom among listed Vietnamese banks. The combined thesis—SBV credit expansion (near-term) + FTSE upgrade (medium-term)—creates a convex payoff profile that justifies concentration rather than distribution across a banking basket.

**Pillar II: Technical Entry Timing.** The strategy employs four independent technical signals to determine optimal entry:

Each signal captures a distinct market dynamic. RSI(14) measures velocity and magnitude of selling pressure—a reading of 44.2 signals exhaustion and statistical mean-reversion expectation. Bollinger Bands provide price context: trading at the Lower Band (2σ below MA20) represents a condition occurring in roughly 2.5% of sessions. Volume at 1.9× the 20-session mean confirms institutional accumulation at the trough rather than noise-driven movement. Proximity to 3-month support at 22,400 limits downside risk to 1.1%. Simultaneously, the portfolio's three winning positions exhibited mirror-image overbought signals—FPT (RSI 68.5, Upper BB), CTR (RSI 81.2, above Upper BB), VTP (RSI 72.4, Upper BB)—confirming that 20/5 represented the maximum spread between liquidation prices and ACB's entry price.

> The optimization strategy rests on the premise that sector-level momentum, when confirmed by technical extremes and volume, identifies the optimal capital destination. Rather than distribute the liberated 377.6 million VND across multiple sectors—diluting returns in a largely negative market—the strategy concentrates it in the single strongest opportunity: ACB at its technical trough.

## III.2. Key Argument — Why ALL-IN ACB, Why Hold?

The strategy deploys the liberated 377.6 million VND into a single position and holds it through the 5/6 close for four reasons:

1. **ACB was purchased at a confirmed technical trough with volume validation.** RSI 44.2 at Lower Bollinger Band with 1.9× volume represents a textbook mean-reversion entry—the joint probability of observing all four signals simultaneously on a random session is sub-0.1%.

2. **The banking sector's momentum was structural, not speculative.** The SBV credit room expansion is a policy instrument with a transmission lag measured in weeks to months, not hours. The full valuation impact materialises across multiple sessions as institutional models update earnings forecasts. Exiting prematurely would abandon the structural catalyst mid-transmission.

3. **A single, well-justified allocation held for 11 sessions carries greater analytical weight than multiple short-term trades.** The strategy submits one falsifiable thesis to the market: that ACB at its technical trough, in the only sector with positive momentum, will outperform. Multiple rapid rotations fragment accountability across sub-decisions with high noise components, making it impossible to distinguish signal from luck.

4. **The strategy is investment, not trading—consistent with the Core-Satellite philosophy of the main report.** The baseline report's Core-Satellite framework prescribes a buy-and-hold posture for structurally justified positions. The ACB allocation is treated as a tactical investment—holding period measured in sessions, but governed by the same discipline of thesis formulation, entry justification, and conviction through the window close.
