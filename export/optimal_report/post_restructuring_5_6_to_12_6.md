## V. Hold Decision — 12/6

### Core rationale:

**1. ACB validates the Core thesis — and catalysts are strengthening.** The only portfolio constituent to gain (+1.3%) during a −2.57% market week. Moody's "Positive" outlook (8/5), Q1 PBT +17% YoY, NPL 0.96%, CAR 12.6%, and the SBV's June 2 directive exempting social housing/industrial park loans from real estate credit caps all point in one direction. Institutional accumulation is confirmed by volume spikes at stable prices (48.8M on 9/6, 61.2M on 12/6). Vietcap TP VND 33,300 (+26%).

**2. FPT/CTR/VTP are correcting on low, declining volume — not distributing.** All three show volume drying up as price declines (FPT: 10.9M → 3.5M; CTR: 434K → 144K; VTP: 494K → 155K). This is seller exhaustion, not distribution. No critical support levels were breached.

**3. Fundamentals have improved since restructuring, not deteriorated.** Since the 20/5 decision: ACB Moody's upgraded to Positive, FPT reported Jan-Apr AI revenue +83.3%, SBV shifted from broad tightening to targeted easing (favoring ACB), and FPT saw ~VND 1,500B foreign net buying during the sell-off. No macro or policy event invalidates the thesis.

**4. The architecture is producing structural alpha.** +1.68pp vs. VN-Index over five sessions is the direct mathematical result of concentrated banking exposure via the strongest name — exactly as designed.

**5. Asymmetric risk/reward favors patience.** Defined stop-losses limit downside to −3% to −5%. Premature trimming would crystallize unrealized mark-to-market losses just as major catalysts approach (rights offering completion by June 18, Q2 earnings in July, FTSE EM in September).
